Making Mathematics Movies

When is a Movie Good? An Axiomatic Approach

Alec Lau

Here I attempt to classify a large amount of definitions and heuristics about what one means when referring to a movie as 'good' or 'great.' I will discuss more specific properties that can follow, their intersections, and, more interestingly, their complements. With each step of the way I will examine the role of subjectivity, taken itself as an axiom. I will attempt to provide the largest amount of examples for each case that does not cloud the structure of this writing.

To avoid a useless semantic discussion on 'what it means to be good (great)', I have compiled a list of properties a good movie should have and what a great movie should have. This is based off of a heuristic but nonetheless intuitive approach that should hopefully encompass most of what is commonly meant by 'good' and 'great.' Note that this is not a proposal of new definitions for the words 'good' and 'great,' but rather classifications that encompass properties that determine whether a movie is 'good' or 'great.'
